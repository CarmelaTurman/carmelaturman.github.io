Tastes Better From Scratch: German Chocolate Cake

Equipment

  • Cake Pans
  • Parchment Paper Rounds

Ingredients

For the Chocolate Cake:

  • 2 cups granulated sugar (400 g)
  • 1 3/4 cups all-purpose flour (210 g)
  • 3/4 cup unsweetened cocoa powder (75 g)
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons baking powder (6 g)
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons baking soda (7.5 g)
  • 1 teaspoon salt (5 g)
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 cup buttermilk (240 ml)
  • 1/2 cup oil (vegetable or canola oil) (120 ml)
  • 2 teaspoons vanilla extract (10 ml)
  • 1 cup boiling water (240 ml)

For the Coconut Frosting:

  • 1/2 cup light brown sugar (100 g)
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar (100 g)
  • 1/2 cup butter (115 g)
  • 3 large egg yolks
  • 3/4 cup evaporated milk (180 ml)
  • 1 Tablespoon vanilla extract (15 ml)
  • 1 cup chopped pecans (120 g)
  • 1 cup shredded sweetened coconut (80 g)

Chocolate Frosting Ingredients

  • 1/2 cup butter (115 g)
  • 2/3 cup unsweetened cocoa powder (65 g)
  • 3 cups powdered sugar (360 g)
  • 1/3 cup evaporated milk (80 ml)
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract (5 ml)

Instructions

  • Prepare Oven: Heat oven to 375°F. Grease two 8 or 9-inch round baking pans. Optionally, cut a round piece of wax or parchment paper for the bottom of the pan to make cake removal easier.
  • For the Cake:
    • Stir together sugar, flour, cocoa, baking powder, baking soda, and salt in a large bowl.
    • In a separate bowl, combine eggs, buttermilk, oil, and vanilla; mix well.
    • Add wet ingredients to dry ingredients and mix to combine.
    • Stir in boiling water (batter will be very thin) and pour into prepared pans.
    • Bake 25-35 minutes (depending on pan size; 9'' pan bakes faster) or until a toothpick inserted in the center comes out clean or with a few crumbs.
    • Cool 5 minutes in the pan, then invert onto wire racks to cool completely.
  • For the German Chocolate Frosting:
    • In a medium saucepan, add brown sugar, granulated sugar, butter, egg yolks, and evaporated milk. Stir to combine and bring to a low boil over medium heat.
    • Stir constantly for several minutes until mixture thickens.
    • Remove from heat and stir in vanilla, nuts, and coconut. Allow to cool completely before layering on cake.
  • For the Chocolate Buttercream Frosting:
    • Melt butter and stir in cocoa powder.
    • Alternately add powdered sugar and milk, beating to spreading consistency.
    • Add additional milk if needed to thin frosting, or extra powdered sugar to thicken. Stir in vanilla.
  • Cake Assembly:
    • Place one cake round on your serving stand or plate.
    • Smooth a thin layer of chocolate frosting over the cake layer, then spoon half of the coconut frosting on top, spreading evenly. Leave about ½ inch from the edge.
    • Stack the second cake round on top and smooth chocolate frosting over the entire cake.
    • Spoon remaining coconut frosting on top of the cake.
