German Chocolate Cake Recipe

Deliciously rich and perfect for every celebration

By Lauren Allen Published on February 13, 2021

Equipment

  • Cake Pans
  • Parchment Paper Rounds

Ingredients

For the Chocolate Cake:

  • 2 cups granulated sugar (400 g)
  • 1 3/4 cups all-purpose flour (210 g)
  • 3/4 cup unsweetened cocoa powder (75 g)
  • 1 1/2 tsp baking powder (6 g)
  • 1 1/2 tsp baking soda (7.5 g)
  • 1 tsp salt (5 g)
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 cup buttermilk (240 ml)
  • 1/2 cup oil (120 ml)
  • 2 tsp vanilla extract (10 ml)
  • 1 cup boiling water (240 ml)

For the Coconut Frosting:

  • 1/2 cup light brown sugar (100 g)
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar (100 g)
  • 1/2 cup butter (115 g)
  • 3 large egg yolks
  • 3/4 cup evaporated milk (180 ml)
  • 1 tbsp vanilla extract (15 ml)
  • 1 cup chopped pecans (120 g)
  • 1 cup shredded sweetened coconut (80 g)

For the Chocolate Frosting:

  • 1/2 cup butter (115 g)
  • 2/3 cup unsweetened cocoa powder (65 g)
  • 3 cups powdered sugar (360 g)
  • 1/3 cup evaporated milk (80 ml)
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract (5 ml)

Instructions

  1. Heat oven to 375°F. Grease two 8 or 9-inch round baking pans. Cut rounds of parchment paper for the bottom of the pans.
  2. For the Cake: Stir together sugar, flour, cocoa, baking powder, baking soda, and salt. In a separate bowl, combine eggs, buttermilk, oil, and vanilla. Add wet ingredients to dry and mix. Stir in boiling water. Pour batter into pans.
  3. Bake for 25–35 minutes or until a toothpick inserted comes out clean. Cool 5 minutes in pans, then invert onto wire racks.
  4. For the German Chocolate Frosting: In a saucepan, combine brown sugar, granulated sugar, butter, egg yolks, and evaporated milk. Stir constantly over medium heat until thickened. Remove from heat and stir in vanilla, nuts, and coconut. Cool completely.
  5. For the Chocolate Frosting: Melt butter, stir in cocoa powder. Alternately add powdered sugar and milk, beating to spreading consistency. Add milk or sugar as needed. Stir in vanilla.
  6. Cake Assembly: Place one cake round on plate. Spread chocolate frosting, then half the coconut frosting. Add second cake round, smooth chocolate frosting over cake, then spoon remaining coconut frosting on top.

Pro Tips

  • Use room temperature ingredients.
  • Run hot water over eggs before mixing.
  • Microwave buttermilk briefly to bring to room temperature.

Make Ahead Instructions

Both frostings can be made ahead and stored in the fridge. Coconut frosting will keep for 1–2 weeks, chocolate frosting for 2–3 weeks.

Wrap cooled cake layers tightly and freeze for up to 3 months. Assemble when ready.

Notes

If baking at high altitude add 3 tablespoons extra flour.
