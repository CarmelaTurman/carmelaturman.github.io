German Chocolate Cake Recipe

Deliciously rich and perfect for every celebration

BY Lauren Allen Published on February 13, 2021

Ingredients

For the Chocolate Cake:

  • 2 cups granulated sugar (400 g)
  • 1 3/4 cups all-purpose flour (210 g)
  • 3/4 cup unsweetened cocoa powder (75 g)
  • 1 1/2 tsp baking powder (6 g)
  • 1 1/2 tsp baking soda (7.5 g)
  • 1 tsp salt (5 g)
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 cup buttermilk (240 ml)
  • 1/2 cup oil (120 ml)
  • 2 tsp vanilla extract (10 ml)
  • 1 cup boiling water (240 ml)

For the Coconut Frosting:

  • 1/2 cup light brown sugar (100 g)
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar (100 g)
  • 1/2 cup butter (115 g)
  • 3 large egg yolks
  • 3/4 cup evaporated milk (180 ml)
  • 1 tbsp vanilla extract (15 ml)
  • 1 cup chopped pecans (120 g)
  • 1 cup shredded sweetened coconut (80 g)

For the Chocolate Frosting:

  • 1/2 cup butter (115 g)
  • 2/3 cup unsweetened cocoa powder (65 g)
  • 3 cups powdered sugar (360 g)
  • 1/3 cup evaporated milk (80 ml)
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract (5 ml)

Instructions

Heat oven to 375°F. Grease two 8 or 9-inch round baking pans. Cut parchment paper rounds for bottom of pans.
This step ensures the cake will not stick and bake evenly.
Stir together sugar, flour, cocoa, baking powder, baking soda, and salt. In separate bowl, mix eggs, buttermilk, oil, and vanilla. Add wet ingredients to dry ingredients and combine. Stir in boiling water. Batter will be thin. Pour into pans.
Make sure boiling water is added slowly and carefully to prevent splattering.
Bake for 25–35 minutes or until toothpick comes out clean. Cool 5 minutes in pans, then invert onto wire racks.
Cooling slightly helps prevent the cake from breaking when removed from pans.
For Coconut Frosting: Combine brown sugar, granulated sugar, butter, egg yolks, and evaporated milk in saucepan. Stir constantly over medium heat until thickened. Remove from heat and stir in vanilla, pecans, and coconut. Cool completely.
Keep stirring to prevent scorching.
For Chocolate Frosting: Melt butter, stir in cocoa powder, alternately add powdered sugar and milk, beating until smooth. Stir in vanilla.
Adjust milk or sugar until frosting is spreadable.
Cake Assembly: Place one cake round on plate. Spread chocolate frosting, then half the coconut frosting. Place second cake round on top. Frost cake with remaining chocolate frosting and top with coconut frosting.
Be generous with frosting for rich flavor.

Pro Tips

  • Use room temperature ingredients for best results.
  • Run hot water over eggs to bring them to room temperature.
  • Microwave buttermilk for a few seconds to warm slightly.

Make Ahead Instructions

Both frostings can be made ahead and stored in the fridge. Coconut frosting lasts 1–2 weeks, chocolate frosting 2–3 weeks. Wrap cooled cake layers tightly and freeze for up to 3 months.

Notes

If baking at high altitude add 3 tablespoons extra flour.
