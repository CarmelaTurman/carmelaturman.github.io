German Chocolate Cake

From Tastes Better From Scratch

Author: Lauren Allen Published:February 13, 2021

Ingredients

  • 2 cups granulated sugar
  • 1¾ cups all-purpose flour
  • ¾ cup unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 1½ teaspoons baking powder
  • 1½ teaspoons baking soda
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 cup buttermilk
  • ½ cup oil (vegetable or canola)
  • 2 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1 cup boiling water
  • ½ cup light brown sugar
  • ½ cup granulated sugar (for frosting)
  • ½ cup butter
  • 3 egg yolks
  • ¾ cup evaporated milk
  • 1 tsp vanilla (for frosting)
  • 1 cup shredded sweetened coconut
  • 1 cup chopped pecans
  • ½ cup butter (for chocolate frosting)
  • ⅔ cup unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 3 cups powdered sugar
  • ⅓ cup evaporated milk (for chocolate frosting)
  • 1 tsp vanilla (for chocolate frosting)

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C). Grease two 8- or 9-inch round baking pans. Line with parchment if desired.
  2. In a large bowl, stir together sugar, flour, cocoa powder, baking powder, baking soda, and salt.
  3. In another bowl, beat the eggs, buttermilk, oil, and vanilla together. Add wet mixture to dry mixture and stir until combined.
  4. Stir in boiling water (batter will be thin). Pour the batter evenly into the prepared pans.
  5. Bake for 25–35 minutes or until a toothpick comes out clean. Let cakes cool in pans for ~5 minutes, then invert onto wire racks to cool completely.
  6. Make the coconut-pecan frosting: In a saucepan, combine brown sugar, granulated sugar, butter, egg yolks, and evaporated milk. Cook over medium heat, stirring constantly until thickened. Remove from heat and stir in vanilla, coconut, and pecans. Cool completely.
  7. Make the chocolate frosting: Melt butter, stir in cocoa powder, then alternately add powdered sugar and evaporated milk until you reach a spreadable consistency. Stir in vanilla.
  8. Assemble the cake: Place one cake layer on a serving plate. Spread a thin layer of chocolate frosting, then top with half the coconut-pecan frosting (leaving a border). Place second cake layer on top. Frost the whole cake with chocolate frosting. Spoon the remaining coconut-pecan frosting on top of the cake.
