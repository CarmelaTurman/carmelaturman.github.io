About the Pacific Rhododendron

The Pacific Rhododendron (Rhododendron macrophyllum) is a striking evergreen shrub native to the Pacific Northwest. Known for its large clusters of deep pink flowers, it blooms from late spring through early summer, creating vibrant landscapes.

Common Names

  • Pacific Rhododendron
  • Bigleaf Rhododendron
  • Western Rhododendron

Size Range

Typically grows between 6 and 12 feet tall, with leaves up to 12 inches long.

Geographic Range

Native to the coastal and mountainous regions of the Pacific Northwest, including Washington, Oregon, northern California, and southern British Columbia.

Preferred Climate

Thrives in mild, wet climates with well-drained acidic soils. Prefers shaded to partially shaded environments.
